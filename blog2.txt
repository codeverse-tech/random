## 🔥 Why Authentication Is Broken in Africa

### **1. Email is not the center of digital life**

In the US or Europe, email is the passport. In Africa, email is often an afterthought. Users prefer phone numbers and WhatsApp. Traditional “email + password” flows are slow and friction-heavy.

### **2. SMS OTP is unreliable and expensive**

* OTP arrives late, or never arrives
* Delivery blocked by carriers
* High cost per message

WhatsApp is near-instant, cheaper, and familiar. But global auth providers ignore this reality.

### **3. Most startups cannot build secure auth**

Authentication is deceptively hard. Early-stage teams often:

* Store passwords insecurely
* Skip rate-limits
* Forget token rotation
* Mis-hash data
* Expose tokens

CredenSync handles security by default.

### **4. Identity APIs aren’t built for African infrastructure**

* Local phone formats
* Delivery quirks
* Dual-SIM numbers
* WhatsApp-first flows

Global providers aren’t optimized for these realities.

## 🛡️ REST vs GraphQL

Authentication is **action-based**, not data-heavy:

* send OTP
* verify OTP
* login
* refresh token
* revoke session

REST is predictable, secure, and SDK-friendly. GraphQL is **optional for admin dashboards**, but **not for core auth**.
